Extra Activities

**Some of the extra activities(relating to robotics) that I have completed this year include...

+++Girl Scouts Gold Award: STEMPOWERED KIDS+++

The issue that I have addressed through my project is to educate more and more girls about the STEM subjects and how girls have an equal chance at gaining a lucrative career in the sciences as boys do. I hope to break the stereotype and current statistics that suggest that men are mostly capable of getting jobs that involve science, engineering, technology, and mathematics. I hope to able to make an impact and benefit the girls that came to my workshops and completed the projects. Workshops such as a class on paper circuits which teaches electronics, a class on chemistry which teaches chemical reactivity and polarity, a class on biology which teaches DNA structure and components, a class on math which teaches problem solving, a class on engineering which teaches structural integrity, and a class on technology which teaches robotics.Even if one girl decides that she is interested in STEM and plans to pursue a career in that direction then I think I have accomplished my goal. Not only girls, but I hope that this helps all younger kids in general. While, my workshops were geared toward girls they were actually open to all. Overall, I hope to change the perceptions around STEM and propel kids, especially girls toward that direction by creating fun, artistic, and rewarding learning activities.
